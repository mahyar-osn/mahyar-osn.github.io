# 👋 Hello, World! I'm Mahyar (/ˈma'h:īär/)

I'm fascinated by intelligence: both the kind that evolved over millions of years in our brains, and the kind we're building in machines. That fascination has taken me on a pretty wild ride through neuroscience, machine learning, and computer vision.

### Right Now

I'm at <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/people/mosanlouy/">Microsoft</a> in Sydney, busy with vision and language AI models. Think computer vision meets natural language processing, with all the interesting challenges that come with making these systems actually work in the real world 🤖.

### The Journey So Far

I've bounced around different corners of AI, always chasing problems that matter:

**<a href="https://www.cotreat.ai/">CoTreat AI</a>**: Built computer vision systems that help dentists spot problems they might miss. Turns out, teaching machines to read X-rays is harder than it looks 🦷.

**<a href="https://provectusalgae.com/">Provectus Algae</a>**: Led the ML team to help discover natural molecules from microalgae. Yes, algae. It's cooler than it sounds 🍀🧬.

**<a href="https://www.auckland.ac.nz/en/abi.html/">Auckland Bioengineering Institute</a>**: Worked at the intersection of AI and bioengineering, which is exactly as interdisciplinary and chaotic as you'd imagine. One of my favourite times in my career that I miss a lot.

**<a href="https://www.soulmachines.com/">Soul Machines</a>**: Helped build digital humans. The experience was unreal and amazing.

**<a href="https://www.aider.ai/">Aider</a>**: Built systems that connect NLP with actual business needs, because what's the point of cool tech if nobody can use it?

### Research & Collaboration

I still collaborate with the Auckland Bioengineering Institute 📚, working with <a href="https://www.google.com/search?gs_ssp=eJzj4tFP1zc0NC5KNk8rqzJg9OIpSC1JLVLIKM0DUgB6bgkw&q=peter+hunter&oq=peter+hunter&gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUqBwgBEC4YgAQyBggAEEUYOTIHCAEQLhiABDIGCAIQIxgnMgcIAxAAGIAEMgcIBBAAGIAEMgcIBRAAGIAEMgcIBhAAGIAEMgcIBxAAGIAEMgcICBAAGIAEMgcICRAAGIAE0gEINzY3MmowajeoAgCwAgA&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8">Prof. Sir Peter Hunter</a> on physics-constrained machine learning. I've recently worked with Oxford's <a href="https://www.mrcbndu.ox.ac.uk/groups/bogacz-group">Bogacz Group</a> on predictive coding - basically trying to understand how our brains predict what's coming next and why that matters for AI.

### Beyond the Screen

When I'm not staring at code or thinking about science, I'm probably on stage 🎭. I've done theatre and film work in Auckland and Sydney. Turns out, understanding human emotion is useful whether you're training neural networks or playing a character. Check out some of my acting work [here](/acting/).
